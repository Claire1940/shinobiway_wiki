## How to Reroll Shinobi Way lineages Efficiently

Rerolling without a plan is where most resources disappear. Build a checkpoint system before spending spins.

### Step-by-step reroll plan

1. **Set a mode goal first**  
   Decide if your next 20–30 hours are PvE grind, bosses, or PvP. This determines your acceptance criteria.

2. **Define a stop rule**  
   Example: “I stop rerolling once I hit one high-value lineage plus one stable secondary.”  
   A stop rule prevents emotional overspending.

3. **Use batch rolling**  
   Roll in sessions (10/25/50), then evaluate. Constant single rolls create bias and tilt.

4. **Test before committing upgrades**  
   Run short route tests: clear speed, survivability, and combo consistency.

5. **Upgrade only after proof**  
   Invest materials once your lineage passes practical tests.

| Account Stage | Recommended Spin Strategy | Stop Condition | Upgrade Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Early game** | Save most spins; roll for consistency | 1 solid all-round lineage | Core skills, mobility |
| **Mid game** | Target role-specific improvements | 1 strong primary + usable secondary | Damage scaling + survivability |
| **Late game** | Fine-tune for mode optimization | High synergy pair for main mode | Min-max stats, niche tech |

A lot of **Shinobi Way lineages** look amazing in clips, but clips don’t show failure states. Always test recovery options after missed abilities.
