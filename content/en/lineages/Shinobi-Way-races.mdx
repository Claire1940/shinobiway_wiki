## How to Obtain and Change Races

In the world of Shinobi Way, your journey usually begins as a standard human (the Shinobi race). However, as you progress and collect resources, you can "spin" for a new lineage. This is typically done through the main menu or specific NPC trainers located in the hidden villages.

To change your race, you generally need **Spins** or **Ryo**. Spins are often distributed via developer codes or earned through daily missions and rank-up rewards. When you use a spin, the game randomly selects a new race for you based on specific rarity percentages.

| Race Name | Rarity Type | Estimated Roll Chance | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Snake** | Rare | 10% | Poison Resistance & Evasion |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a backup of at least 50 spins before trying to change a Rare race. You don't want to get stuck with a Common race if you fail to roll a Legendary one!

## Detailed Breakdown of Shinobi Way Races

Each of the **Shinobi Way races** offers a distinct playstyle. While some players prefer the raw power of the Celestial, others find the utility of the Shark or Snake races more beneficial for specific dungeon runs or territorial wars.

### The Shinobi (Human) Race
The baseline for all players. While it doesn't offer flashy passives, it provides the most "blank slate" experience, allowing your gear and Bloodlines to define your build without interference. In 2026, many top-tier players actually stick with Shinobi if they are running highly specialized builds that don't require specific race-based elemental buffs.

### The Shark Race
The Shark race is the king of mobility. If you find yourself fighting near water or in maps with large lakes, the Shark race provides a massive passive boost to swimming speed and reduces stamina consumption while sprinting. 

*   **Water Walk Efficiency:** 50% less stamina used.
*   **Health Regen:** Slightly boosted while in contact with water.
*   **Best For:** Open-world exploration and escaping high-pressure PvP situations.

### The Snake Race
The Snake race focuses on being "slippery." It provides a natural boost to your evasion frames (i-frames) during dashes. Furthermore, it grants a 25% resistance to poison-based status effects, which are prevalent in many high-level boss fights in the 2026 update.

### The Puppet Race
A more technical choice, the Puppet race is designed for players who enjoy using tools and long-range projectiles. It increases the damage of all "Ninja Tool" items and adds a small "stagger" effect to basic ranged attacks, making it difficult for enemies to close the distance.

## The Celestial Race: The 2026 Apex

The Celestial race remains the most sought-after among all **Shinobi Way races**. With a mere 1% drop rate, it represents the pinnacle of genetic evolution in the game. Characters of this race often sport white or glowing hair and unique eye patterns that signify their divine lineage.

| Feature | Celestial Bonus |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Health Points** | +15% Base HP |
| **Chakra Pool** | +20% Total Chakra |
| **Movement** | Hovering/Enhanced Jump |
| **Special Ability** | Passive "Divine Aura" (Minor DMG Reduction) |

Players aiming for the Celestial race should look for "Full Moon" events within the game, as these limited-time windows sometimes double the roll chance for Legendary lineages.

## Strategic Comparison for Combat

Choosing between the **Shinobi Way races** isn't just about rarity; it's about synergy. If your primary Bloodline focuses on close-range combat, the Shark race's stamina management might be more useful than the Puppet race's ranged buffs.

| Build Type | Recommended Race | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Melee/Brawler** | Shark | High stamina allows for constant pressure and combos. |
| **Zoner/Caster** | Puppet | Increases tool damage and keeps enemies at bay. |
| **Tank/Survival** | Snake | Evasion and poison resistance are key for longevity. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Changing your race will overwrite your current race benefits immediately. There is no "undo" button once a spin is confirmed, so choose wisely!

## Progression and Evolution

As of the mid-2026 update, some races can now be "Evolved" through specific questlines. For example, a Shark race player can undergo the "Deep Sea Ritual" to unlock the "Great White" variant, which further enhances health regeneration and adds a bleed effect to physical strikes.

To begin an evolution, you must:
1. Reach Level 1000 with your current race.
2. Collect 5,000,000 Ryo.
3. Defeat the specific Race Boss (e.g., The Serpent King for Snake evolution).

These evolutions are essential for endgame content, particularly the "God-Tier" raids that were introduced earlier this year. The evolution process doesn't change your base race but adds a secondary layer of powerful passive skills.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best race for new players in Shinobi Way?**

A: For beginners, the Shark race is highly recommended. Its stamina buffs make traveling between quest objectives much faster and less taxing on your resources, allowing you to level up more efficiently than the standard Shinobi race.

**Q: How often are new Shinobi Way races added to the game?**

A: The developers typically introduce a new race or a major evolution path every 4 to 6 months. In 2026, we have already seen the expansion of the Puppet and Celestial lineages, with rumors of a "Cyborg" race coming in the late-year update.

**Q: Can I keep my race if I prestige my character?**

A: Yes, in the current 2026 version of the game, your race is tied to your character slot and will persist through prestige resets. You will not lose your Celestial or Shark status when you reset your level to 1.

**Q: Is there a way to guarantee a Legendary race roll?**

A: There is no 100% guaranteed way to roll a Legendary race. However, using "Pity Spins" (available after 500 unsuccessful rolls) significantly increases your chances of landing an Epic or Legendary result.
