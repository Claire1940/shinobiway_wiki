## Mastering the Shinobi Way How to Play: Kicks and Bashes

Mobility is the heart of the Shinobi experience. Unlike static heroes, Shinobi thrives on "Double Dodges" and "Front Rolls" to reposition and open up defenses. His kicks are his primary bashes, and knowing when to use them is the difference between a successful combo and being guard-broken.

### The Dodge Kick (Opener)
By moving left, right, or forward and double-tapping your dodge input, you can transition into a kick. This bash confirms a heavy attack on hit. However, be cautious; if you miss and attempt a backflip immediately, you may be vulnerable to a guaranteed Guard Break (GB).

### The Front Roll and Flip Kick
After connecting with a heavy attack or a dodge forward heavy (Ghoul's Affliction), you can perform a **Front Roll**. From this roll, you have several options:
1.  **Flip Kick:** A bash that leads to a guaranteed 15 damage heavy (Ghoul's Torment).
2.  **Sickle Rain Finisher:** An unblockable top heavy or undodgeable side heavy.
3.  **Guard Break:** Catching opponents who expect a kick and try to dodge.

## Advanced Defensive Mechanics: Deflects and Parry Counters

A true master of the **Shinobi Way how to play** doesn't just attack; they use the opponent's momentum against them. Shinobi possesses one of the most unique deflects in [For Honor](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/for-honor), characterized by an automatic teleport.

*   **Teleport Deflect:** Upon a successful deflect, Shinobi teleports behind the enemy for **20 damage**. You can immediately follow this up with a **Spectral Kick** by pressing the bash button, leading into further mixups.
*   **Parry Counters:** After a parry, pressing the light attack button performs a "Light Repulse" for 14 damage. Like the deflect, this can lead into a Spectral Kick or a backflip to reset the neutral game.

> **⚠️ Warning:** While the teleport deflect is powerful, it is a preset animation. Ensure you are ready to input your follow-up (Kick or Heavy) immediately to maintain pressure.

## Strategy: The Art of Sickle Rain

The "Sickle Rain" is Shinobi's signature move. It is triggered by landing a chain finisher (the unblockable top or undodgeable side heavy) and then inputting three light attacks in a specific rhythm.

This move pins the opponent, making it incredibly powerful in team fights. While you are pinning an enemy with Sickle Rain, your teammates can land heavy attacks for massive coordinated damage. However, be aware that you are also vulnerable during this animation. If an enemy teammate is nearby, they can hit you out of the Sickle Rain, so always check your surroundings before committing to the full three-hit light sequence.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Shinobi good for beginners in 2026?**

A: Shinobi has a steep learning curve due to his low health pool and reliance on "Double Dodges." However, once you master the **Shinobi Way how to play** basics, he becomes one of the most rewarding heroes to play because of his high mobility.

**Q: How do I stop getting guard-broken after a kick?**

A: If you miss a kick, do not immediately input a backflip if you suspect the opponent is waiting for it. You can sometimes delay your follow-up or simply take the reset to neutral to avoid the guaranteed GB.

**Q: What is the best way to use Super Sprint?**

A: Super Sprint is excellent for rotating between capture points in Dominion. Use it to reach a fight quickly, but always keep an eye on your stamina bar. If you arrive at a fight with zero stamina, you become an easy target.

**Q: Can I faint the Sickle Rain finishers?**

A: Yes. Both the unblockable top heavy and the undodgeable side heavies can be fainted into a Guard Break or a light attack to catch opponents who are trying to parry or dodge.
