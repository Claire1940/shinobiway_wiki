## Initial Character Customization and Spinning

Before you even step foot into the hidden villages, your journey begins on the customization screen. This is where you define your ninja's identity and, more importantly, their initial power set. You are given a set of starting spins that allow you to roll for Elements and Alignments.

When tweaking your character, pay close attention to your **Speed** stat and your **Alignment**. While hair and clothing are purely cosmetic, your starting element—such as Fire or Water—will dictate your early-game grinding efficiency. If you aren't satisfied with your initial element, use your starting spins to try for a rarer lineage.

| Customization Option | Impact on Gameplay | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **Element** | Determines your primary jutsu attacks. | Aim for **Fire** for high DPS or **Water** for versatility. |
| **Alignment** | Affects your passive bonuses and social standing. | Spin until you land a rare or legendary tier. |
| **Speed Stat** | Influences movement and dodge velocity. | Keep this balanced; 14 is a solid starting point. |
| **Hair/Aesthetics** | Purely visual. | Customize to your preference; doesn't affect stats. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't spend all your spins immediately. Save a few for after you've reached Level 10, as you may want to pivot your build once you understand the combat flow.

## Mastering the Core Controls

Shinobi Way utilizes a complex control scheme that rewards players who can manage their Chakra effectively. Unlike standard action games, movement here is vertical and fluid. You must learn to "draw" your Chakra to perform advanced maneuvers like wall-breaking and water-walking.

| Keybind | Action | Strategic Use |
|---|---|---|
| **C** | Draw/Charge Chakra | Essential for fueling jutsu and specialized movement. |
| **M** | Open Menu | Access inventory, elements, stats, and settings. |
| **F** | Defense/Block | Reduces incoming damage; vital for boss fights. |
| **Shift** | Sprint | Use for traversing the map and dodging wide-area attacks. |
| **X** | Special Jump/Wall Climb | Allows you to scale vertical surfaces using Chakra. |
| **Ctrl** | Toggle Walk/Run | Useful for precision platforming and stealth. |

To move like a true master, you must learn to put Chakra into your feet. This allows you to break through certain environmental barriers and reach hidden mission NPCs located on high rooftops or secluded cliffs.

## Early Game Progression: Levels 1 to 10

Your first major milestone is reaching **Level 10**. Before this point, many of your elemental abilities remain locked. To level up quickly, you should focus on low-risk missions found within the starting village. 

The mission system is categorized by colored icons. For a new player following this **Shinobi Way setup guide**, the Blue Star missions are your best friend. These usually involve simple tasks like finding a lost cat or delivering items. For example, the "Find the Cat" mission rewards you with 70 coins and 90 experience points—a significant boost for a Level 1 character.

### Stat Allocation Strategy
As you gain levels, you will earn stat points. For beginners, a balanced approach is usually the safest. Many top players recommend putting points into **Defense** early on. A higher defense pool allows you to survive mistakes during combat encounters. A common early-game distribution is a 1:1 ratio between Defense and your primary Elemental stat.

## Mission Types and Rewards

As you grow stronger, you can move away from Blue Star missions and toward the Scroll missions. These provide better rewards but involve combat against NPC ninjas.

| Mission Icon | Difficulty | Typical Objective | Rewards |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Blue Star** | Very Easy | Fetch quests (e.g., finding pets). | Low Coins, Low XP. |
| **Green Scroll** | Easy/Medium | Defeating basic thugs or scouts. | Moderate Coins, Moderate XP. |
| **Red Scroll** | Medium/Hard | Defeating waves of enemies or a mini-boss. | High XP, Chance for items. |
| **Orange Scroll** | Hard | Defeating elite ninjas or bosses. | **Katana Drops**, High Coins. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt Red or Orange scrolls alone if you are under Level 15. Use the "Allies" menu to invite other players to your party to share the burden of combat.

## Unlocking Rock Lee’s Fighting Style

One of the most sought-after combat upgrades in the early game is the Rock Lee fighting style. This style focuses on high-speed melee combos and physical prowess rather than just elemental jutsu. 

To find the trainer, head toward the large lake. Look for a section with metal grates near the water's edge. Follow the path straight ahead until you see a large rock formation in the middle of the lake. The trainer resides there. However, simply finding him isn't enough. You must meet the following requirements:
1.  **Level 25:** You cannot learn this style until you've mastered the basics.
2.  **100,000 Coins:** The training fee is steep, so save your mission rewards.

If you are short on coins, remember that you can sell unwanted items in the shop or complete daily missions, such as defeating 20 enemies, to earn extra currency and spins.

## Advanced Tactics: Boss Farming and The Katana

Once you have reached Level 25 and perhaps unlocked a fighting style, it is time to hunt for gear. The Orange Scroll missions have a chance to drop the **Katana**, a powerful melee weapon that drastically increases your damage output. 

A popular boss spawn is located near the waterfall. To get there, exit the main village and take the first right. Cross the bridge on your left that sits directly in front of the waterfall. You can use your Chakra jump (X) to scale the cliffs to the boss arena.

### The "Kite" Strategy
If you find the boss too difficult to fight head-on, use the environment to your advantage. Lead the boss toward a narrow passage or a cliff edge. From a safe distance, "flood" the boss with ranged elemental attacks like Fireball or Water Bullets. This "kiting" method allows lower-level players to defeat high-level bosses without taking direct hits. Once defeated, check the ground for a yellowish-orange scroll—this is your ticket to a new sword.

## Settings for Performance
Shinobi Way is a visually intensive game. If you experience lag or frame drops, navigate to the **M (Menu)** and then to **Settings**.
*   **Performance Mode:** Turn this ON if you have a lower-end PC.
*   **Music/SFX:** Can be toggled to help you focus on combat cues.
*   **PvP Toggle:** If you are just trying to farm missions, keep PvP OFF to avoid being "ganked" by higher-level players.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more spins in Shinobi Way?**

A: You can earn spins by completing daily missions (like playing for 15 minutes), using promotional codes found in the game's social media groups, or purchasing them from the NPC **Faltern Tage** for 4,400 coins each. Following a **Shinobi Way setup guide** helps you maximize your coin gain to buy these spins.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm XP early on?**

A: The starting village is the most efficient spot until Level 10. Focus on the Green Scroll missions once you have unlocked your first elemental attack, as they provide a better XP-to-time ratio than the Blue Star fetch quests.

**Q: Can I change my element after starting the game?**

A: Yes. You can go back to the customization menu from the main screen and use your saved spins to roll for a new element. Note that this will replace your current element, so choose wisely.

**Q: What is the benefit of joining a group?**

A: Joining the official game group often unlocks the ability to use exclusive codes and may provide a passive XP or coin boost. It also makes it easier to find allies for the harder Red and Orange scroll missions.
