## How to Defeat the Shinobi Hunter Enshin of Misen

The Shinobi Hunter Enshin of Misen, found in the Hirata Estate, serves as a major skill check. He utilizes long-range spear thrusts that can quickly deplete your health. Before engaging him, ensure the Mikiri Counter is unlocked in your skill tree. This fight is a perfect example of the **Shinobi Way how to beat bosses** through preparation and environmental awareness.

### Step 1: Clear the Perimeter
Do not rush directly at the boss. He is surrounded by several guards who will overwhelm you if you are spotted. Use the nearby trees and tall towers to survey the area.

1.  **Use the Branch:** Locate the tree branch near the bridge to reach the top of the tall tower.
2.  **Stealth Drop:** Crouch and drop near the edge of the flowers to remain undetected.
3.  **Target the Archer:** The archer is your primary threat during the initial phase. Sneak up and eliminate him first.
4.  **Eliminate the Torch Enemy:** Quickly dash to the enemy holding the torch and take him out before he can alert others.
5.  **Bait the Axe Guard:** Lure the slow-moving axe enemy toward the bridge. Use charged attacks to interrupt his heavy swings.

### Step 2: The Boss Engagement
Once the "small fries" are dealt with, focus entirely on Enshin of Misen. The **Shinobi Way how to beat bosses** like this involves staying at a medium distance to bait out his thrusting attacks.

> **💡 Tip:** Do not spam the dodge button. Wait for the red "Perilous Attack" kanji to appear, then time your Mikiri Counter (Circle/B button) just as the thrust is about to connect.

## Essential Combat Tactics for Success

Understanding the flow of combat is the most important part of the **Shinobi Way how to beat bosses**. You should never be passive. If you are not deflecting, you should be attacking. This pressure prevents the boss from recovering their posture.

### Posture vs. Vitality
While some bosses have massive health pools, their posture often regenerates slower as their health drops. In the early stages of a fight, focus on landing a few hits to lower their vitality. Once their health is around 60-75%, their posture will recover much slower, making it easier to land a finishing Deathblow.

| Boss State | Strategy | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| **High Health** | Vitality Chipping | Use combat arts and prosthetic tools |
| **Mid Health** | Posture Pressure | Maintain constant aggression and deflections |
| **Broken Posture** | Execution | Perform the Red Circle Deathblow immediately |

> **Warning:** Hesitation is defeat. If you back away too often to heal, the boss will recover all the posture damage you worked so hard to build.

## Stealth and Environmental Mastery

A true shinobi uses the environment to even the odds. Many bosses allow for a "Stealth Deathblow" to start the fight, effectively removing 50% of their total health before the real combat begins.

1.  **Crouch Walk:** Always approach a boss arena from the shadows or high ground.
2.  **Ledge Hangs:** Use ledges to move behind bosses without making noise.
3.  **Smoke and Cover:** Use tall grass to break line of sight if a fight goes poorly.

When applying the **Shinobi Way how to beat bosses**, always look for a way to get that first stealth hit. In the case of the Shinobi Hunter, you can sneak through the flowers to get a backstab, leaving him with only one health bar for the remainder of the duel.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important skill for the Shinobi Way how to beat bosses?**

A: The Mikiri Counter is widely considered the most essential skill. It allows you to counter thrust attacks, which are common among many of the game's hardest bosses. Without it, you are forced to dodge perfectly, which is much riskier and provides less of an advantage.

**Q: Why is my posture breaking so quickly?**

A: Your posture breaks because you are likely blocking rather than deflecting. A block (holding the button) still builds posture damage for you. A deflection (tapping the button at the exact moment of impact) builds posture damage for the enemy and prevents your own gauge from breaking.

**Q: Can I beat bosses without using prosthetic tools?**

A: Yes, every boss can be defeated using only your sword. However, tools like firecrackers or the loaded axe make certain encounters significantly easier by exploiting specific enemy weaknesses.

**Q: How do I stop a boss from regenerating posture?**

A: You must lower their vitality (health) first. The lower a boss's health is, the slower their posture recovers. Stay aggressive and ensure you are landing hits during their recovery animations to keep the pressure on.
