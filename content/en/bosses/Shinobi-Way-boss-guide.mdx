Navigating the treacherous paths of the Hirata Estate requires more than just raw strength; it demands the cunning and precision of a true master. Successfully navigating the Hirata Estate requires a solid **Shinobi Way boss guide** to overcome the early difficulty spikes that often discourage new players. This **Shinobi Way boss guide** focuses specifically on the Shinobi Hunter Enshin of Misen, a formidable opponent who guards the path to the Main Hall. As one of the first major skill checks in the game, this encounter teaches players the absolute necessity of rhythmic deflection and specialized counter-moves. By understanding the environment and the boss's predictable but punishing spear thrusts, you can turn a frustrating wall into a showcase of shinobi prowess.

## Essential Skills for the Shinobi Way Boss Guide

Before you even consider stepping onto the bridge leading to the Shinobi Hunter, you must ensure your skill tree is properly developed. The most critical component of any **Shinobi Way boss guide** is the acquisition of the **Mikiri Counter**. This skill is found in the Shinobi Arts tree and is arguably the most important combat maneuver in the entire game for dealing with thrust attacks.

The Mikiri Counter allows you to stomp an opponent's weapon into the ground when they perform a perilous thrust attack (indicated by a red kanji symbol). Without this skill, you are forced to dodge perfectly or take massive posture damage. With it, you turn the boss's greatest strength into their greatest weakness, dealing massive posture damage to them instead.

| Skill Name | Tree | Importance | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mikiri Counter** | Shinobi Arts | **Mandatory** | Counters thrust attacks for high posture damage. |
| **Whirlwind Slash** | Shinobi Arts | Recommended | Useful for clearing out the smaller enemies nearby. |
| **Suppression (Stealth)** | Shinobi Arts | Optional | Makes the initial stealth kill significantly easier. |

> **💡 Tip:** Practice the Mikiri Counter on the undead soldier at the Dilapidated Temple before heading to Hirata Estate to get the timing down.

## Preparing the Battlefield: Stealth and Add Management

The area surrounding the Shinobi Hunter is filled with "small fries"—archers, torch-wielders, and axe-men—who will quickly overwhelm you if you rush in blindly. A professional **Shinobi Way boss guide** emphasizes that the fight begins long before you lock onto the boss. You must systematically clear the area to ensure a one-on-one duel.

Start by using the environment to your advantage. Use the branches to get on top of the tall tower overlooking the bridge. From this vantage point, you can scout the enemy positions. Crouch and drop into the flowers to avoid detection. Your first priority should be the archer positioned near the boss. Archers can interrupt your counters and provide the boss with openings to kill you instantly.

| Enemy Type | Priority | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Archer** | **High** | Kill immediately after the first stealth blow on the boss. |
| **Torch Enemy** | **Medium** | Fast but fragile; take them down with quick slashes. |
| **Axe Enemy** | **Low** | Slow attacks; use charged attacks or the Loaded Axe to break them. |

Once you have positioned yourself, crouch-walk toward the Shinobi Hunter. If you are careful, you can land a stealth deathblow, immediately removing one of his two health spheres. As soon as the animation ends, ignore the boss for a moment and sprint to the archer and torch enemies. Dispatch them quickly and then retreat toward the bridge to bait the axe enemy away from the boss.

## Shinobi Hunter Enshin of Misen Move Set

Once the area is clear, the real duel begins. Enshin of Misen uses a spear, giving him significant range advantages. This **Shinobi Way boss guide** recommends staying at a medium distance—close enough to bait his thrusts, but far enough to react to his sweeps.

The core of this fight is timing your "B" (Xbox) or "Circle" (PlayStation) button press. When you see the red kanji and the spear begins to move forward, dodge *into* the attack. This triggers the Mikiri Counter. If you dodge to the side or backward, you will likely be caught by the spear's tracking.

| Attack Pattern | Type | Counter Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Thrust** | Perilous | **Mikiri Counter** (Dodge toward the enemy). |
| **Spear Sweep** | Perilous | Jump over the spear and kick his head for posture damage. |
| **Multi-hit Combo** | Physical | Deflect each hit rhythmically; watch for the final thrust. |
| **Grab/Poke** | Physical | Quick side-step and follow up with a charged attack. |

It usually only takes two or three successful Mikiri Counters to completely fill his posture meter. Once the meter is red, hit him with a few standard sword strikes to ensure he doesn't recover, then perform the final Shinobi Execution.

## Advanced Tactics and Prosthetic Tools

While the Mikiri Counter is the star of the show, utilizing your prosthetic tools can make the journey through the "Shinobi Way" much smoother. The Hirata Estate is where you find several key tools that are useful for this fight and the ones that follow.

The **Flame Vent** is particularly effective against the enemies leading up to the boss, as many are unarmored and susceptible to burn status. However, for the boss himself, the **Loaded Axe** is a better choice if you struggle to find openings. The axe deals massive posture damage and can hyper-armor through some of the boss's lighter attacks.

> **Warning:** Do not attempt to use the Loaded Axe while the boss is preparing a thrust attack. The thrust will almost certainly hit you before the axe lands, leading to a quick death.

## Mastering the Shinobi Way Boss Guide Flow

To summarize the encounter for those looking for a quick reference, follow these steps in order:

1.  **Unlock the Mikiri Counter** in the Shinobi Arts tree before entering the Bamboo Thicket Slope.
2.  **Clear the bridge** and the path leading to the tower using stealth.
3.  **Use the tower** to drop down and stealth-attack the Shinobi Hunter for his first health bar.
4.  **Eliminate the archer** and torch enemies immediately while the boss recovers.
5.  **Bait the thrusts** by standing at a medium distance.
6.  **Execute the Mikiri Counter** by dodging into the red kanji thrusts.
7.  **Stay aggressive** to prevent his posture from regenerating.

By following this **Shinobi Way boss guide**, you transform a chaotic multi-enemy encounter into a controlled, tactical victory. The Shinobi Hunter is designed to teach you that in this world, defense is often your best offense. Mastering the Mikiri Counter here will serve as the foundation for your success against much more difficult bosses later in the year 2026.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is the Shinobi Way boss guide focused so much on the Mikiri Counter?**

A: The Mikiri Counter is the specific "hard counter" for the Shinobi Hunter Enshin of Misen. Because he primarily uses a spear for thrusting attacks, using this skill is the fastest and most reliable way to break his posture. Without it, the fight is significantly longer and more dangerous.

**Q: I keep failing the Mikiri Counter timing. What am I doing wrong?**

A: Most players fail because they dodge too early or try to dodge to the side. You must dodge directly toward the enemy right as the thrust is about to connect. Wait for the "flash" of the spear tip or the moment the kanji begins to fade.

**Q: Can I skip the smaller enemies and just fight the boss?**

A: It is not recommended. The archers have a very long line of sight and will shoot you while you are locked in animations, which usually leads to a quick death. Clearing the "small fries" first ensures you can focus entirely on the boss's move set.

**Q: What reward do I get for defeating the Shinobi Hunter?**

A: Defeating him rewards you with a Prayer Bead, which is essential for increasing your maximum Vitality and Posture. He also drops a significant amount of Experience Points for early-game progression.
