For those who prefer speed, deception, and high-mobility glass-cannon builds, mastering the **Shinobi Way weapon skills** is the only path to survival. Unlike the Samurai, who focus on heavy armor and grounded defense, the Shinobi relies on perfect timing and a vast arsenal of gadgets to turn the tide of battle.

## How to Unlock Skills in Nioh 3

Before you can dive into the complexities of the ninja skill tree, you must understand the currency of progression. Nioh 3 utilizes a "Locks of Hair" system to grant skill points. These items are consumed to gain points that can be spent in the Martial Arts or Ninjutsu menus.

| Item Type | Usage | Common Acquisition Methods |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Samurai Locks** | Unlocks Samurai-specific weapon skills | Main story quests, Treasure chests, Dojo missions |
| **Ninja Locks** | Unlocks Shinobi Way weapon skills and Ninjutsu | Shooting down Chio, Side missions, Hidden corpses |
| **Skill Respec** | Resets all spent points | Central menu (any time), Blacksmith (special items) |

You won't be starved for these items if you explore the maps thoroughly. In 2026, the game is designed to encourage experimentation, allowing you to respec your build at almost any time if a particular weapon style isn't clicking for you.

## Essential General Shinobi Way Weapon Skills

The Ninja form in Nioh 3 plays significantly differently than its predecessors. One of the biggest shifts is how you manage Ki (stamina). While Samurai use the Ki Pulse to recover energy, Ninjas must stay in motion to keep their resources high.

### The Evade Skill
This is arguably the most important skill in the entire Ninja arsenal. Unlike a standard dodge, the **Evade** skill functions as a "perfect dodge." When you time your movement to narrowly avoid an attack, you will see a signature yellow flash. This flash signals a massive Ki restoration and grants you Ninjutsu points.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ninjas do not naturally restore Ki with standard dodges. Without the Evade skill, you will quickly find yourself winded and vulnerable.

### Mid-air Dodge and Foot Stool Jump
To truly master the verticality of Nioh 3, you must unlock **Foot Stool Jump** followed by **Mid-air Dodge**. This allows you to leap off enemies and reposition while in the sky. When combined with the Evade skill, you can even dodge projectiles in mid-air to restore Ki, making you nearly untouchable.

## Mastering the Shinobi Way Weapon Skills Tree

While general movement is vital, your specific weapon choices define your offensive pressure. The **Shinobi Way weapon skills** provide unique ways to stagger enemies and create openings for devastating backstabs.

### Drifting Counter (Dual Ninja Swords)
If you are using the Dual Ninja Swords, the **Drifting Counter** is a mandatory unlock. By holding back and the heavy attack button, your character enters a ready stance. If an enemy strikes during this window, you automatically parry the blow and teleport behind them. Since Ninjas deal significantly higher damage from behind, this skill is a primary tool for melting boss health bars.

### Trembling Earth
For those who find themselves surrounded by mobs, **Trembling Earth** is the answer. This skill releases a burst of energy that staggers all nearby enemies. The most effective way to use this is as an aerial follow-up; jump over an enemy with Foot Stool Jump, then activate Trembling Earth as you descend to create a safe landing zone.

| Skill Name | Weapon Type | Primary Benefit | Stance Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drifting Counter** | Dual Swords | Automatic parry and reposition | Any |
| **Sword Ki** | Katana | High Ki damage AoE burst | High Stance |
| **Trembling Earth** | Any (Ninja) | Staggers groups / Aerial viable | Mid/High |
| **Backstab** | All | Massive damage to unaware targets | Passive |

## Ninjutsu Tools and Gadgets

A true master of the Shinobi Way doesn't rely solely on steel. Your weapon skills should be complemented by Ninjutsu items that debuff enemies or provide crowd control.

1.  **Gunpowder Bombs:** These are your primary source of fire damage. They are excellent for inflicting the "Burn" status, which deals damage over time and lowers enemy defense.
2.  **Caltrops:** Use these to control the battlefield. Scattering caltrops slows down aggressive enemies, allowing you to pick them off with ranged attacks or prepare a heavy combo.
3.  **Uncanny Flame:** This passive ability increases the effectiveness of all fire-based tools, making your bombs and fire-buffed weapons even more lethal.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the enemy's elemental weaknesses. Using a Gunpowder Bomb on a Yokai weak to fire can end a fight before it even begins.

## Advanced Combat: Flux and Stance Dancing

Even though the Ninja form emphasizes evasion, you should not ignore the Samurai's **Flux** and **Flux II** skills. These abilities allow you to recover extra Ki by switching stances during a Ki Pulse. While Ninjas use Evade for primary recovery, learning to weave Flux into your offensive rotations ensures you never run out of energy during a long boss encounter.

## Section Title: Optimizing Your Shinobi Build

When building around **Shinobi Way weapon skills**, focus your stat points on Dexterity and Skill. Dexterity directly increases your Ninjutsu power and the damage of your Ninja-specific weapons, while Skill improves your technical efficiency and Ki recovery speeds.

| Stat | Priority | Impact on Shinobi Way |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dexterity** | High | Increases Ninjutsu damage and capacity |
| **Skill** | High | Increases Dual Sword damage and Ki recovery |
| **Heart** | Medium | Increases base Ki pool |
| **Strength** | Low | Minimal impact on Ninja weapons |

By focusing on these stats, you ensure that every strike from your **Shinobi Way weapon skills** hits with maximum impact. Remember to keep your equipment weight under 30% to maintain the "A" rank agility, which is required to make the most of the Evade and Mid-air Dodge mechanics.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use Shinobi Way weapon skills while in Samurai form?**

A: No, the skills are tied to your specific combat form. However, passive bonuses unlocked in one tree often provide benefits to your overall character stats, and some weapon skills (like those for the Katana) may have versions in both trees.

**Q: What is the best weapon to pair with the Shinobi Way?**

A: The Dual Ninja Swords are widely considered the best choice due to the Drifting Counter skill and their high hit rate, which applies status effects like Burn or Poison very quickly.

**Q: How do I get more Ninja Locks in 2026?**

A: Beyond main missions, look for "Chio" (small mechanical birds) perched in high places. Shooting them down with a ranged weapon almost always rewards you with a Ninja Lock or high-tier crafting materials.

**Q: Is the Deflect skill useful for a Ninja build?**

A: While the Ninja focuses on evasion, the Samurai's **Deflect** skill is still worth unlocking. It provides a reliable backup defense and generates Ninjutsu points for your Ninja form when performed successfully.
